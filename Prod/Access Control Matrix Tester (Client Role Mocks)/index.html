üéì Access Control Security Principles

Principle of Least Privilege: Users should only have the minimum permissions necessary to perform their tasks.
Role-Based Access Control (RBAC): Permissions are assigned to roles, and roles are assigned to users.
Separation of Duties: Critical operations should require multiple users or approval workflows.
Defense in Depth: Multiple layers of access control should be implemented.
Regular Access Reviews: Permissions should be regularly audited and updated.
